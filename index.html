About

Hi, I'm Céline.

— 🌿 —

Before I became a driving instructor, I spent years working as a registered nurse — across Australia and internationally. That background shapes everything about how I teach. I know how to read anxiety, how to explain things clearly under pressure, and how to keep someone calm when they're outside their comfort zone. Those aren't small things when you're learning to drive.

I built Gentle Path around a single belief: that the way you're taught shapes the driver you become. Calm instruction produces calm drivers. Patient teaching produces confident ones.

South Australia Licensing

Your Path to a Full Licence

I help students at every stage — from first L-plate lesson through to VORT preparation and beyond.

1
L

Getting Your Ls

Complete the 2-part theory test at Service SA — Part A (8 give way questions) and Part B (32 of 42 multiple choice). Then you can start driving with a qualified supervisor.

2
P

Earning Your P1s

  • Hold your Ls for at least 12 months
  • Log 75 hours (including 15 at night)
  • Complete CBT&A log book or pass VORT
  • Pass the Hazard Perception Test at Service SA
3
P

Moving to P2

After 12 months on your P1, apply for your P2 licence. P-plates no longer need to be displayed, though most provisional conditions still apply for the 2-year P2 period.

4
Full

Full Licence

After 2 years on your P2 licence, you can apply for your full, unrestricted South Australian driver's licence. Safe and happy driving!

Licensing requirements are set by the SA Government and may change. Always check sa.gov.au for current requirements.

Code of Conduct

Our Commitment to You

Gentle Path Driving Instruction is committed to providing a safe, respectful, and professional learning environment for every student. We uphold the standards set by ADTA SA — the Australian Driver Trainers Association of South Australia.

Instructor Standards

  • All lessons are delivered with patience, respect, and without harassment or discrimination of any kind.
  • The instructor will never raise their voice, use abusive language, or create an intimidating environment.
  • The instructor maintains current NPC and DCSI clearances and will provide copies to parents on request.
  • The instructor will not use a mobile phone while the vehicle is in motion.
  • Lessons are conducted in a fully insured, roadworthy, dual-controlled vehicle.

Student Expectations

  • Students are expected to treat their instructor with respect at all times.
  • Students must hold a current SA learner's permit (or provisional licence where applicable).
  • Students must not be impaired by alcohol, drugs, or medication that affects driving ability.
  • Students are expected to be punctual and to notify the instructor as early as possible of any changes.

Safeguarding

Gentle Path Driving Instruction takes child safety seriously. All lessons with students under 18 are conducted in the dual-controlled vehicle only, with the dash cam active. Parents are welcome to contact the instructor at any time with concerns.

Complaints

Any concerns or complaints should be directed to the instructor in the first instance. If unresolved, complaints may be escalated to ADTA SA.

Privacy Policy

Gentle Path Driving Instruction ("we", "us", "our") is committed to protecting your personal information in accordance with the Australian Privacy Act 1988 and the Australian Privacy Principles.

What We Collect

We collect personal information including your name, phone number, email address, home suburb, date of birth (for log book purposes), and learner permit number. We may also collect information about your learning needs and progress.

Why We Collect It

This information is collected solely for the purpose of delivering driving instruction services, maintaining log book records, scheduling lessons, and communicating with you about your bookings.

How We Store It

Your information is stored securely. We do not share your personal information with any third party except where required by law or with your explicit consent (e.g. log book data submitted to Service SA).

Dash Cam Footage

Our vehicle is equipped with a dash cam for safety purposes. Footage is automatically overwritten after a period of time unless retained for a specific incident. Footage will not be shared without consent except where required by law or an insurance claim.
